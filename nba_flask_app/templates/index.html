NBA Analytics Project

Game 4 of the 1987 NBA Finals: Magic Johnson "Junior Skyhook"



For my final project, I wanted to know what stats affect a basketball team's ability to win or lose a game. Based upon my initial research, I found that Dean Oliver identified "Four Factors of Basketball Success". He based this on shooting(40%), turnovers(25%), rebounding(20%) and free throws(15%). The NBA measures this using the stats eFG%, TOV%, OREB%, and FTRate respectively. These factors can be expanded to the opposing team as well to give a more accurate prediction which is what the "fourfactor" model uses for it's model predictor features.

Although my initial model was adequately accurate, I wanted to improve upon this model so I decided to use some of the four factors with additional parameters. Recently, pace of play has become an emphasis in the NBA. When I tried to use this in my, "mymodel" predictor, I found that the pace had little impact on improving the accuracy so I ended up experimenting with other parameters. I eventually found that a team's defensive rating gave better accuracy in predictions. I also used TS% instead of eFG% which takes into account three point field goal percentage and free throws. Based upon these changes I was able to improve my model score from 0.9351(Four Factor Model) to 0.9775(My Model).

After presenting my project, I also wanted to add an ability to predict the score of games. To do this, I used multiple regression models to experiment. In order to predict scores, I fit my regression models to the previous season data similar to what was done for my win/loss predictor. Additionally, since I needed to predict a score I need to develop a method to input parameters into my model. In addition, I wanted to be able to get a feel for how good a team was at the current moment. In order to do this I added a new feature of ELO score for the team. The ELO score was taken from the previous season and was carried forward to the current. After each game I also updated the ELO score. Additionally, I wanted to be able to capture the home/road factor for teams as well as current past performance for these instances. Thus when I predicted scores I looked at performance for home and road games as well (using an average of the past five road or home game depending on match up) and fed my model based on these additional factors. Since I know that predicting score is difficult I was hoping to see if I could more accurately predict the point spread versus the Vegas odd predictions. I started to scrape odds data for match ups as well for comparison. Thus far my models seem to predicted somewhat well for point spread and with a bit more work on feature selection and model tuning could be a viable tool for betting.
